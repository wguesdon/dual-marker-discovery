# Research Track — Judging Criteria

Two-stage judging. Stage 1 is async (judges score independently on standardized criteria, Tue–Wed July 14–15). Stage 2 is a live final round (Thu July 16) where pre-recorded demos play and judges deliberate. Same weighted criteria decide both.

The weights are the same across tracks. What changes is what "good" looks like for a Research project. Below is each criterion with the Research-track reading.

## Weighted criteria

### 1. Impact — 25%
"Is this a finding or analysis others can build on? Does it fit your track's problem statement?"

For a Research project this is the core question. The bar is not a demo that works, it is a result with a truth value. A nominated variant, a trained model, a reproducible analysis. Show who can build on it and why it matters. Tie it explicitly back to the "Build From the Bench" problem statement: started from a real biological question, found the datasets and tools, produced something discrete.

### 2. Claude Use — 25%
"How creatively did this team use Claude Code? Did they go beyond a basic application? Did they surface capabilities that surprised even us?"

Show the work Claude Science / Claude Code actually did in getting to the result, not just that it was used. Concrete: which datasets it found, which analysis steps it drove, where it went beyond a single prompt. The phrasing rewards surprising capability, so lead with the least obvious thing it did.

### 3. Depth & Execution — 20%
"Did the team push past their first idea? Is the engineering sound and thoughtfully refined? Real craft, not just a quick hack?"

Evidence of iteration. The SatMut project is literally a pivot from the CD4 approach, so the narrative of "first idea → why it did not hold → what replaced it" is directly on point here. Sound method: dual oracle, the Spearman gate, validation rather than a ranked candidate list.

### 4. Demo — 30%
"Is this a working, compelling demo? Does the project hold up — findings you trust? Is it genuinely cool to watch?"

Highest single weight. Three-minute max, pre-recorded, and the same video carries into the live final round. For a research result "working demo" means the analysis runs and the finding is legible on screen. The animation and the live base-by-base annotation are the watchable payload. Make the trustworthiness visible: show the gate being passed, not just the conclusion.

## Weighting at a glance

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| Demo | 30% |
| Impact | 25% |
| Claude Use | 25% |
| Depth & Execution | 20% |

Demo + Impact together are 55%. A trustworthy, watchable result that others can build on wins more than engineering polish.

## Gladstone Institutes Award — $10k usage credits

Hand-selected by the Gladstone team for the research project with the most potential to advance the field. Separate from the ranked prizes. Judged on scientific potential, so the strength of the finding itself matters most here.

## Pre-submission checklist

- [ ] Result has a truth value settled by the deadline (a nomination, a model, a reproducible number) — not a ranked candidate list.
- [ ] Repo is open-source under an approved license and public.
- [ ] Demo video is under 3 minutes and shows the finding, not just the pipeline.
- [ ] Video shows the validation gate being passed, so judges can trust the result.
- [ ] Written summary is 100–200 words and names the biological question up front.
- [ ] Claude Science's actual contribution is called out concretely.
- [ ] Analysis was done during the event; only the question and public datasets predate it.
- [ ] Submitted on the CV platform before July 13, 9:00 PM ET.
